Read before you size

The class number is the badge rate in CFH at ½″ w.c. for 0.6 SG natural gas. Capacity at any other drop is badge × √(ΔP ÷ 0.5).

Air m³/h mode multiplies the stamped air rating by 45.6 (35.3 m³→ft³ × 1.29 air→0.6 SG gas) to get gas CFH. Use it when the badge reads "RATED CAP. (AIR)".

Going ½″ → 1″ buys +41%, not double. Half the meter's badge gets you √2, not 2×.

Size continuous load to the badge rate. Higher-differential capacity is short-peak headroom — every extra inch of drop is pressure you lose downstream on a 7″ w.c. system.

Diaphragm meters only. Ultrasonic units (Intelis, etc.) have a fixed Qmax and near-zero drop — this √ law does not apply to them.

How-To

Reading the Meter Badge

Where to find the number to enter for meter capacity, shown on the two badge styles you’ll see in the field.

What you’re looking for

Find the line with the word AIR — e.g. 5 m³/h AIR. On a Canadian badge, an air rating is the ½″ w.c. capacity by definition. Enter that number in Air m³/h mode and the app converts it (×45.6) to gas BTU/h.

Some older badges also stamp a class number (e.g. AL M 225) — that’s the gas CFH rating. Enter it in Gas CFH mode. Either entry gives the same answer.

Example 1 — modern badge (Sensus / Enbridge)
RATED CAP. (AIR) 5 m³/HR → enter 5 in Air m³/h mode

The “0.125 kPa DIFF” on the same line is ½″ w.c. spelled out in metric — confirmation you’ve got the right line.

Example 2 — older badge (Consumers Gas)
5.1 m³/h AIR → enter 5.1 in Air mode · or AL M 225 → enter 225 in CFH mode
Badge checklist

✓ Capacity labelled AIR (m³/h) — that’s the ½″ w.c. figure
✓ TEMP. COMP. 15°C — Canadian standard conditions
✓ Measurement Canada approval (G- or AG- number)
✗ Don’t enter the dm³/rev number — that’s displacement, not capacity
✗ Don’t enter the kPa MAOP — that’s the housing pressure rating
✗ Serial numbers and model codes (MR-8) are not capacities

Field shortcut: m³/h AIR × 45 ≈ gas capacity in thousands of BTU/h. 5 → ~225k.
